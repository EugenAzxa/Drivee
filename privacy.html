PRIVACY · DRIVEE

Your data stays yours.

Drivee is built so the people who park in Toronto don't trade their privacy for help with a parking ticket. This page explains exactly what we keep, what we don't, and how to wipe everything.

LAST UPDATED · 6 MAY 2026

✓ THE SHORT VERSION

The only thing we save on our servers about you is your email address — and only if you choose to sign in to sync your reminders across devices.

Everything else — your tickets, your scans, your photos, your plate, your name — lives on your device. We don't read it. We don't sell it. We can't see it.

What we save

Your email address (only if you sign in)

If you choose to sync Drivee across your phone, tablet, and laptop, you sign in with a magic link sent to your email. We store that email so we can recognise you next time. That's it. No password, no name, no phone number, no payment information.

Anonymous usage events

We count things like "someone scanned a ticket" or "someone opened the dispute builder" so we know which features people use and which to improve. These events don't include your email, your plate, your name, or anything that can identify you.

What we don't save

  • Your tickets and reminders. Stored in your browser's local storage — on your device only. If you sign in to sync, an encrypted copy lives in our cloud database tied to your email — only your account can read it.
  • Photos of your tickets. When you scan a ticket, the photo is processed in your browser and only the extracted text leaves your device — and only to read what's on the ticket. We never store the image.
  • Your name, plate, address. Saved locally so the app remembers you between visits. Never sent to our servers unless you sign in to sync.
  • Your location. Used live by the GPS Guardian feature to warn you about hotspots. Never logged, never sent anywhere.
  • Your payment information. Drivee is free. We never collect card details from you. Ticket payments happen on the City of Toronto's own payment portal — we just deep-link you there.

Who else sees your data

Nobody. We don't sell your data. We don't trade it for ad money. We don't share it with the City of Toronto, the MTO, the courts, or any law firm listed in the app.

The lawyers in our directory pay flat fees for placement — they don't get any visitor data from us. When you call or email a lawyer through the app, you do that directly with them; we don't sit in the middle.

How to delete everything

  • On your device: Open the Drivee app → tap your avatar → "Sign out and clear local data". This wipes every reminder, scan, and profile detail from your phone or browser.
  • From our cloud: Email drivee.canada@gmail.com with the subject "Delete my account" from the email you signed in with. We delete your row from our database within 24 hours and confirm by reply.

Children

Drivee is intended for licensed Ontario drivers (18+). We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has used the app, email us — we'll delete their data immediately.

Changes to this policy

We'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page when we change anything material. Significant changes will also be flagged inside the app.

TERMS OF USE · DRIVEE

Plain-English terms.

Drivee is a tool, not legal advice

Drivee helps you organise parking and traffic fines. The dispute scripts, AI verdicts, and ROI calculations are informational tools to help you decide — not legal advice. We are not your lawyer. For complex tickets (DUI, stunt driving, plate denial, fines over $200), use the lawyer directory in the app to talk to a real licensed paralegal or lawyer.

Drivee is free

Free for life, free to scan, free to dispute. We make money when lawyers in our directory get retained — never from you. There is no subscription, no premium tier, no ads.

You own your tickets

You're responsible for paying or disputing your tickets in the time the city allows. Drivee surfaces deadlines, but if you ignore them and your fine escalates, that's between you and the City of Toronto.

The app may break

We try to keep the app running, but we make no guarantee of uptime. If a deadline alert fails to fire, that's not a basis to dispute a fine — the city's official notice always wins. Always read the official notice you received.

Trademarks

Drivee is not affiliated with the City of Toronto, the Toronto Parking Authority, the MTO, the Ontario Court of Justice, or any private parking enforcement company. Logos and names belong to their owners.
